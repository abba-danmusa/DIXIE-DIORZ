About Dixie - Diorz

We began as boys in blue trousers and white shirts — young, restless, and often unaware of the gift we were receiving. Hassan Ibrahim Gwarzo Secondary School was strict. Tough. Demanding. At the time, it felt heavy. Discipline was non-negotiable. Standards were high. Excuses were few.

Like most teenagers, we resisted it.

Years later, life did what life always does — it revealed the truth gently, and sometimes painfully. What once felt harsh became clear. What once felt restrictive became formative. The discipline, the balance between Western and Islamic education, the values quietly instilled in us — they shaped our character long after we left the school gates.

The idea of giving back had always lived among us, quietly. In small ways — helping one another, showing up when it mattered. But it was the loss of one of our own, Auwal Ahmad Aliyu, that awakened something deeper.

Auwal was more than a friend; he was a brother. His passing left a void that words could not fill. In our grief, we found purpose. We realized that our shared experiences, our education, and the values instilled in us could be a force for good. Thus, the Dixie - Diorz Foundation was born — a tribute to Auwal's memory and a commitment to uplift others as we had been uplifted.

His passing reminded us of life’s fragility — and of responsibility.

Guided by the Islamic teachings we received at HIGSS, we chose to act. We carried out humanitarian work in Auwal’s name, seeking Sadaqatul Jariya — a charity whose reward continues even after death. It was our way of honouring him. Our way of praying for him. Our way of turning grief into goodness.

In that moment, the purpose became clear.

The Dixie-Diorz Foundation was born from faith, loss, gratitude, and unity. Rooted in the lessons of our past and driven by the needs of the present, we exist to serve society at large — to help the vulnerable, uplift the forgotten, and stand for compassion, as our Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) commanded in his final sermon.

This foundation is not about one person. It is not about recognition. It is about togetherness.

Because if the Dixie-Diorz Foundation succeeds, it means we all succeed together — as one.

And in serving others, we continue the legacy that shaped us — quietly, sincerely, and with purpose.

Our Mission

To serve humanity with compassion and responsibility, inspired by faith and guided by values. We exist to uplift the vulnerable, support education and personal growth, and transform kindness into meaningful action — so that no one is left behind.

Humanitarian Support

We extend compassion to families facing hardship, providing food, essentials, and relief. Because dignity belongs to every person, and kindness given at the right moment can change a life.

Community Growth

We keep the bonds of the Class of 2012 alive, turning shared memories into collective action. Together, we support one another and give back to the community that raised us.

Emergency Assistance

Life happens to all of us. When a member of our family faces hardship, we show up with love, support, and resources — because community means never facing storms alone.

Annual Outreach

Once a year, we come together — alumni, volunteers, friends — to give back in the loudest, brightest way. A day of unity, compassion, and lives touched forever.
